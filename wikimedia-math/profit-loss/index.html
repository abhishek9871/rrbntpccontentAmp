Profit and Loss

Basic Definitions

Profit and loss calculations are fundamental concepts in mathematics that deal with the financial aspects of buying and selling goods.

Key Terms

Cost Price (CP)

The amount for which an item is bought. It is the base amount from which profit percentage is calculated. In a transaction, this is the amount an item is purchased for.

Example: If something is bought for $40, then Cost Price (CP) = $40

Selling Price (SP)

The price at which an item is sold. It is also referred to as 'revenue' or 'sales' and forms the base for profit margin calculation.

Example: If something is sold for $100, then Selling Price (SP) = $100

Profit

The gain made when selling price is greater than cost price.

Formula: Profit = Selling Price - Cost Price

Example: If Cost Price = $40 and Selling Price = $100, then Profit = $100 - $40 = $60

Loss

The amount lost when selling price is less than cost price.

Formula: Loss = Cost Price - Selling Price

Profit Percentage

The percentage of cost price that one gets as profit on top of cost price.

Formula: Profit Percentage = 100 × (Profit / Cost Price)

Example: If Cost Price = $40 and Selling Price = $100

Profit = $100 - $40 = $60

Profit Percentage = (60/40) × 100 = 150%

Loss Percentage

The percentage of cost price that represents the loss.

Formula: Loss Percentage = 100 × (Loss / Cost Price)

Example: If Cost Price = $100 and Selling Price = $80

Loss = $100 - $80 = $20

Loss Percentage = (20/100) × 100 = 20%

Profit Margin

A financial ratio that measures the percentage of profit earned in relation to revenue. It indicates how much profit is made for every dollar of revenue generated.

Formula: Profit Margin = 100 × (Profit / Revenue)

Alternative Formula: Profit Margin = 100 × (Sales - Total Expenses) / Revenue

Types of Profit Margins

Gross Profit Margin

Calculated as gross profit divided by net sales (percentage). Gross profit is calculated by deducting the cost of goods sold (COGS) from revenue.

Formula: Gross Profit Margin = 100 × (Gross Profit / Net Sales)

Gross Profit Formula: Gross Profit = Revenue - (Direct materials + Direct labor + Factory overhead)

Net Sales Formula: Net Sales = Revenue - Cost of Sales Returns - Allowances and Discounts

Operating Profit Margin

Includes the cost of goods sold and is the earning before interest and taxes (EBIT) known as operating income divided by revenue.

Formula: Operating Profit Margin = 100 × (Operating Income / Revenue)

Net Profit Margin

Net profit divided by revenue. Net profit is calculated as revenue minus all expenses from total sales.

Formula: Net Profit Margin = 100 × (Net profit / Revenue)

Markup

Synonymous with 'profit percentage', indicating it is the percentage of cost price that one gets as profit on top of cost price.

Formula: Markup = 100 × (Selling Price - Cost Price) / Cost Price

Discount

A deduction from the regular price or marked price. Discounts are often mentioned as deductions from revenue when calculating 'Net Sales'.

Formula: Discount Amount = Marked Price - Selling Price

Discount Percentage: Discount Percentage = (Discount Amount / Marked Price) × 100

Solved Examples

Example 1: Basic Profit Calculation

Problem: A shopkeeper buys a watch for $200 and sells it for $250. Find his profit and profit percentage.

Solution:

  • Cost Price (CP) = $200
  • Selling Price (SP) = $250
  • Profit = SP - CP = $250 - $200 = $50
  • Profit Percentage = (Profit/CP) × 100 = (50/200) × 100 = 25%

Example 2: Loss Calculation

Problem: A man buys a bicycle for $1500 and sells it for $1200. Find his loss and loss percentage.

Solution:

  • Cost Price (CP) = $1500
  • Selling Price (SP) = $1200
  • Loss = CP - SP = $1500 - $1200 = $300
  • Loss Percentage = (Loss/CP) × 100 = (300/1500) × 100 = 20%

Example 3: Profit Margin Calculation

Problem: A company has revenue of $500,000 and total expenses of $350,000. Calculate the profit margin.

Solution:

  • Revenue = $500,000
  • Total Expenses = $350,000
  • Profit = Revenue - Expenses = $500,000 - $350,000 = $150,000
  • Profit Margin = (Profit/Revenue) × 100 = (150,000/500,000) × 100 = 30%
